Motivated by Bo “Know-Grind” Williamson, Virginia “Suga” Lee agrees to lead a student government sponsored performance arts competition to raise funds for Autism Awareness and Suicide Prevention.  Influenced by music producer and teammate, Orlando “Summa-Time” Caine, they develop a winning strategy for planning the event.

 

Opposed by a competing spoken word group, deviously orchestrated by Alexias Griffin, their team suffers humiliation through tactical sabotage, disruptively damaging individual reputations, while threatening critical HBCU inclusion.

 Subsequently, facilitated by Orlando “Summa-Time” Caine’s uncle, the team receives coaching and mentoring through popular industry artists and clergy members.   Renewing their strategic plan, they proceed with unprecedented efforts engaging nationwide communities and students representing colleges and universities.
