This engaging summary presents an analysis of Replay by Marc Levy, which follows the New York Times journalist Andrew Stilman, who is stabbed and left for dead by a mystery assailant while out jogging one morning. When he wakes up, he realises that he has gone back in time to two months earlier, and resolves to use the second chance he has been granted to find and stop his attacker, and to repair his fractured relationship with his high-school sweetheart. Along the way, he is forced to reevaluate his views on love, honesty and truth, and comes to an important realisation about the meaning of life. Marc Levy is one of France's most popular contemporary authors, having published a string of international bestsellers, including If Only It Were True and P.S. from Paris.
