The Mathematics of Faith is a book about the convergence between the diverse fields of science and spirituality; mathematics and faith. Contrary to the view held by many that the two worlds are mutually exclusive protagonists, this amazing book shows that the God of heaven is in fact the author of both. Any sincere individual who ventures to go deeper into either world will discover the other.

In twelve chapters this book introduces the idea of The Mathematics of Faith and explores the relationship of Biblical faith with mathematical fields like the law of averages, probability, geometry and power. The author tackles the glory of God and how it is perceived by a mathematical mind. Throughout the book the author uses simple mathematical illustrations to help readers understand the dynamics of biblical faith.

This is a universal book and church leaders, Bible teachers and indeed, people from all walks of life should find message of The Mathematics of Faith a great help.
